H1-B Visa for Non-immigrant Workers US 2011-2016


H1-B visa is a temporary employment-based visa granted to non-US citizen individuals who are hired for specialty occupations that require special skillset and ability. Only 65,000 visas are granted each year to qualified professionals and this application will provide you an overall view of non-immigrant workforce from 2011 – 2016.


What is this app?

This app will present multiple analyses about visa H1-B application for non-immigrant labor during the period from 2011-2016, especially the data science workforce. Based on history records, this app will also provides a prediction tool which will take inputs about the visa application and predict case approval likelihood.


Who can use this app?

Are you:

  • interested in applying for H1-B visa in data science?
  • employer who need a frame of reference in determining case’s approval likelihood for your prospective employees based on history records?
  • simply interested in seeing the trend in non-immigrant employment over the past 5 years?

If you answer 'yes' to any of the question above, look no further, you've come to the right place.


How to use this app?

This application uses H1-B visa data from Department of Labor (DOL) from 2011 - 2016.

Exploratory Data Analysis: On this page, you will find multiple graphs which will help you answer the following questions:

  1. How many H1-B visa applications were filed during the period of 2011 through 2016?
  2. How did the status of these applications look like?
  3. Who were the most active employers in hiring and sponsoring H1-B visa for non-immigrant workers?
  4. What were the most demanding jobs for non-immigrant workers in the market from 2011-2016?
  5. Which companies hired the most non-immigrant data scientists?
  6. Did geographic differences have any influence on data science hiring?
  7. How did data scicentist salaries vary by states?

Visa Prediction: An H-1B visa can be denied when the applicant does not meet the required knowledge or skills for the offered job. Or jobs with lower wage are more likely to require less specialized skills which could lead to higher rejection rates in order to preserve domestic labor. If you have information about your job such as job group, annual salary, time commitment, you are welcome to utilize this tool to predict your case approval likelihood.
